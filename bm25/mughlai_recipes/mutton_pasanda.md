# Shahi Mutton Pasanda
A luxurious, velvet-textured Mughlai specialty, Shahi Mutton Pasanda features flattened, ribbon-like strips of tender mutton leg meat, heavily marinated in an almond-yogurt paste and slow-braised to perfection.

## Place of Origin
Lucknow and Bhopal, India.

## Backstory
The name "Pasanda" is derived directly from the Urdu word *Pasand*, which means "favorite" or "choice." Historically, this dish was reserved for Mughal emperors who demanded only the prime cuts of meat. The royal butcher would selectively carve out large, flat, boneless slices exclusively from the leg of a lamb (*Raan*). These premium strips were flattened into thin sheets using wooden mallets before cooking, ensuring an elite texture that absorbed royal spices effortlessly.

## Ingredients
### Main Components
* **Mutton:** 600g Boneless Mutton Leg cuts, sliced thin and flattened into 3x4 inch ribbons
* **Fat Source:** 4 tbsp Asli Ghee

### The Rich Pasanda Marination
* **Dahi (Yogurt):** 1 cup, thick and well-strained
* **Badam Paste (Almond Paste):** 2.5 tbsp (Blanched almonds ground smooth)
* **Adrak-Lahsun Paste:** 1.5 tbsp
* **Kachri Powder:** 1 tsp (Traditional wild melon powder used for tenderizing meat)
* **Shahi Jeera (Caraway Seeds):** 1 tsp
* **Garam Masala Powder:** 1 tsp
* **Haldi Powder:** 1/2 tsp

### Gravy Base & Aromatics
* **Pyaz (Onions):** 2 large onions, fried deep golden brown (crushed into Birista)
* **Choti Elaichi:** 4 pods
* **Laung:** 4 pieces
* **Zafran (Saffron):** A generous pinch soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk

## Preparation
1. **Flattening the Meat:** Lay the thin strips of mutton boneless meat on a clean chopping board. Using a meat mallet or the flat back of a heavy knife, gently pound the meat until it spreads flat and drops to half its original thickness.
2. **Marination Process:** In a wide mixing bowl, merge the strained dahi, badam paste, adrak-lahsun paste, kachri powder, shahi jeera, garam masala, haldi, and salt. Coat the flattened mutton pasandas extensively in this blend. Let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 5 hours.

## Cooking Steps
1. **Aromatic Base:** Heat asli ghee in a heavy-bottomed flat pan or handi. Add the whole green cardamoms and cloves, allowing them to swell up for 15 seconds.
2. **Braising the Flaps:** Carefully slide the flat, marinated strips of mutton into the hot ghee, ensuring they lay flat without folding over excessively. Pour any remaining marinade over the top. Cook on high heat for 5 minutes, turning the meat flaps once to sear both sides.
3. **The Slow Simmer:** Reduce the flame to low. Mix in the hand-crushed fried onion birista. Cover with a heavy lid and allow the pasandas to slowly simmer in their own moisture for about 40 minutes.
4. **Saffron Finish:** Once the meat feels completely soft and velvety, pour the saffron-infused warm milk across the surface of the gravy. Cook uncovered for 5 minutes until the sauce reduces to a thick, coating consistency that clings tightly to the meat ribbons.

## When to Eat
* **Occasions:** Prepared for high-end celebratory dinners, family milestones, and imperial-themed daawats.
* **Meal Timing:** Best served as a main dish for an indulgent, slow-paced weekend dinner.

## With What to Eat
* **Accompaniments:** Pairs beautifully with thin *Mughlai Paratha*, soft *Rumali Roti*, or hot *Baarkhani Roti*.
* **Condiments:** Serve alongside a refreshing platter of mint leaves, sliced lime, and cucumber ribbons.

## Chef's Secrets
* **The Kachri Advantage:** Authentic Pasanda relies heavily on *Kachri Powder* (wild melon) for tenderizing. If kachri is completely unavailable in your spice market, replace it with raw papaya paste, but exercise caution—papaya can over-tenderize thin strips of pounded meat into mush if left for too long.
* **Pounding Technique:** When flattening the meat, do not hit it too hard with the mallet. The objective is to gently break the muscle fibers and flatten the strip uniformly to absorb marinades, not to puncture holes or tear the delicate meat ribbon apart.
