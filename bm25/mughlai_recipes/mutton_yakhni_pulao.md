# Shahi Mutton Yakhni Pulao
An aristocratic Mughlai rice delicacy, Shahi Mutton Yakhni Pulao features long-grain Basmati rice cooked entirely within a deeply aromatic, clear, spice-infused mutton broth (*Yakhni*), yielding an elegant, subtle flavor profile.

## Place of Origin
Awadh (Lucknow) and Delhi, India.

## Backstory
Unlike the robust and heavily layered Biryani, Yakhni Pulao traces its direct heritage back to ancient Persian courts, arriving in India with the early Mughal rulers. The core culinary objective of a Pulao was absolute subtlety—every single grain of rice had to absorb the complete essence of the meat broth while maintaining a clean, pale cream look without any visible spices or grease. It was the preferred choice of the royal courts during formal diplomacy lunches.

## Ingredients

### Main Components
* **Mutton:** 750g bone-in mutton pieces (large juicy cuts from the leg and shoulder)
* **Rice:** 2.5 cups Long-grain Basmati Rice, soaked in water for 30 minutes
* **Cooking Fat:** 4 tbsp Asli Ghee

### The Yakhni Broth Potli (Tie in a muslin cloth cheesecloth)
* **Sabut Dhania (Whole Coriander Seeds):** 2.5 tbsp
* **Saunf (Fennel Seeds):** 2 tbsp
* **Pyaz:** 1 whole onion, peeled and cut into halves
* **Lahsun:** 1 whole garlic bulb, unpeeled and washed
* **Adrak:** 2-inch piece, smashed
* **Khada Masala:** 2 bay leaves, 4 cloves, 4 green cardamoms, 1 cinnamon stick

### The Pulao Assembly
* **Pyaz:** 1 large onion, finely sliced
* **Dahi (Yogurt):** 1/2 cup, whisked
* **Garam Masala Powder:** 1/2 tsp
* **Kewra Water:** 1 tsp

## Preparation
1. **The Muslin Potli:** Place all the ingredients listed under the Yakhni Broth Potli section into a clean square piece of cheesecloth or muslin fabric. Tie the corners tightly to create a clean, secure spice pouch (*Potli*).
2. **Rice Soaking:** Wash the Basmati rice gently until the water runs completely clear of starch, then soak for 30 minutes.

## Cooking Steps
1. **Extracting the Yakhni:** In a large pot, combine the mutton pieces, 5 cups of water, salt, and the spice *Potli*. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer on medium-low heat for 45 minutes until the mutton is cooked through and tender.
2. **Straining:** Remove the spice pouch and press it hard with a spoon to extract all the concentrated juices back into the broth. Fish out the mutton pieces and place them in a bowl. Strain the remaining liquid through a fine sieve. This clear, fragrant broth is your *Yakhni*. Measure it—you need exactly 4 cups of broth.
3. **Sautéing the Meat:** In a separate heavy-bottomed deep pot (*Degh*), heat asli ghee. Sauté the sliced onions until they turn a soft golden color. Add the cooked mutton pieces and whisked dahi. Cook on medium heat for 5 minutes until the yogurt blends in completely.
4. **Boiling Rice in Broth:** Pour the 4 cups of clear measured Yakhni broth into the pot. Bring it to a boil. Drain the soaked Basmati rice completely and add it to the boiling liquid. Add a tiny pinch of garam masala.
5. **The Dum Stage:** Cook on high heat uncovered until the rice absorbs most of the broth and tiny holes form on the surface of the rice bed. Seal the pot with a tight lid. Reduce the flame to the absolute lowest setting and let it cook on *Dum* (slow steam) for 15 minutes. Sprinkle kewra water right before serving.

## When to Eat
* **Occasions:** A sophisticated alternative to Biryani for royal lunches, elegant dinner celebrations, and traditional afternoon daawats.
* **Meal Timing:** Highly favored as a primary dish for an upscale family lunch.


## With What to Eat
* **Accompaniments:** Best enjoyed with thin, cool *Zeera Raita* or a spicy whole-red-chilli garlic chutney to add a sharp kick.
* **Condiments:** Serve alongside a simple salad of sliced red onions and crisp mint leaves.


## Chef's Secrets
* **Broth to Rice Ratio:** The exact math of a Yakhni Pulao lies in the liquid ratio. For every 1 cup of soaked Basmati rice, you require exactly 1.5 to 1.75 cups of clear Yakhni broth. If you add too much broth, your rice grains will turn mushy and stick together; if too little, the core will remain hard.
* **The Potli Cleanness:** Never skip the muslin cloth pouch and just throw the whole spices directly into the water. If you do, the coriander and fennel skins will darken the mutton broth and get stuck in the rice, destroying the clean, pristine ivory aesthetic of an authentic royal pulao.
