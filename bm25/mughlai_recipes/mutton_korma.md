# Shahi Mutton Korma
The pinnacle of Mughlai festive dining, Shahi Mutton Korma is a rich, aromatic, and deeply flavorful gravy characterized by its unique grainy texture, achieved through slow-cooking meat with fried onions, yogurt, and a paste of nuts.

## Place of Origin
Agra and Delhi, India.

## Backstory
Mutton Korma has documented roots dating back to the 16th century in the grand court of Emperor Akbar. The word "Korma" is derived from the Turkish word *Kavurma*, which means to braise or slow-cook. Royal chronicles state that this specific dish was prepared by the imperial chefs to celebrate the inauguration of the Taj Mahal complex, cementing its position as the ultimate celebratory centerpiece dish across the Mughal empire.

## Ingredients
### Main Components
* **Mutton:** 750g bone-in mutton pieces (mix of raan/leg and chaamp/ribs)
* **Ghee:** 1/2 cup Asli Ghee

### The Gravy Base
* **Pyaz (Onion):** 3 large onions, sliced razor-thin for even frying
* **Dahi (Yogurt):** 1 cup, smooth and well-whisked (not sour)

### Whole Khada Masala
* **Tejpatta (Bay Leaf):** 2 pieces
* **Dalchini (Cinnamon):** 1-inch stick
* **Laung (Cloves):** 6 pieces
* **Choti Elaichi (Green Cardamom):** 6 pods
* **Javitri (Mace):** 1 blade

### Spice Powders & Aromatics
* **Adrak-Lahsun Paste:** 2 tbsp
* **Dhania Powder (Coriander):** 2 tbsp
* **Kashmiri Lal Mirch Powder:** 1.5 tsp
* **Haldi Powder (Turmeric):** 1/2 tsp

### Shahi Finishers
* **Kaju-Badam Paste:** 2 tbsp (Cashews and blanched almonds ground together)
* **Kewra Water:** 1 tsp
* **Jaiphal (Nutmeg) Powder:** A tiny pinch

## Preparation
1. **Birista Creation:** Heat the asli ghee in a heavy-bottomed copper handi or deep pot. Fry the sliced onions on medium heat until they turn a perfect uniform golden brown. Remove immediately, spread them on paper towels to crisp, and then crush them coarsely with your hands once cooled. Do not grind into a paste.
2. **Yogurt Mix:** In a separate bowl, mix the coriander powder, Kashmiri red chilli powder, and turmeric powder directly into the whisked dahi to prevent the yogurt from curdling later during cooking.

## Cooking Steps
1. **Braising the Meat:** In the same ghee used for frying onions, add the whole khada masalas (tejpatta, dalchini, laung, elaichi, javitri). Let them splutter for 20 seconds. Add the mutton pieces and adrak-lahsun paste. Sauté vigorously on high heat for 5-7 minutes until the meat changes color from pink to a light brown (*Bhunao* stage).
2. **Simmering with Dahi:** Turn the heat down to low. Add the spiced dahi mixture slowly while stirring continuously. Cook for 5 minutes until the oil or ghee begins to separate from the sides.
3. **Slow Cooking:** Add 1.5 cups of warm water, cover the handi with a heavy, tight-fitting lid, and slow-cook on low heat for about 45-50 minutes until the mutton becomes exceptionally tender.
4. **Building Texture:** Stir in the crushed fried onions (birista) and the kaju-badam paste. Cook uncovered for 10 minutes on low heat. You will notice the gravy instantly thickening and adopting a distinct grainy, rich texture.
5. **The Shahi Touch:** Sprinkle the nutmeg powder and drizzle the kewra water over the korma. Cover the pot and turn off the heat, letting the dish trap the floral aromas for 5 minutes before opening.

## When to Eat
* **Occasions:** The premier main course dish for wedding banquets, heavy family daawats, Eid celebrations, and formal weekend dinners.
* **Meal Timing:** Highly satisfying when consumed as a heavy lunch or an indulgent dinner dish.

## With What to Eat
* **Accompaniments:** Pairs beautifully with traditional *Khamiri Roti*, *Sheermal* (saffron-infused sweet flatbread), or long-grain *Zafrani Pulao*.
* **Condiments:** Serve with fresh mint sprigs and soft cucumber raita to offset the heavy, rich profile of the gravy.

## Chef's Secrets
* **The Birista Trap:** Never let your fried onions turn dark brown or black. Overcooked onions will ruin the entire dish by imparting a bitter taste and giving the korma an undesirable dark color instead of a rich, golden-amber hue.
* **Grainy Texture Secret:** The hallmark of a great Korma is its *Danedar* (grainy) gravy. This is achieved exclusively by using hand-crushed fried onions and incorporating spices directly into the dahi. Grinding fried onions in an electric mixer turns the gravy into a smooth paste, which completely destroys the authentic korma structure.
