# Shahi Mutton Boti Kebab
A classic Mughlai tandoori delicacy, Mutton Boti Kebab features tender, bite-sized cubes of lamb marinated in an intensely spiced, raw papaya-infused yogurt mixture, skewered and grilled over charcoal.

## Place of Origin
Delhi (Old Delhi/Shahjahanabad), India.

## Backstory
Mutton Boti Kebab evolved as a popular street food variant of grand royal tandoori dishes in the bustling lanes of Old Delhi surrounding Jama Masjid. While whole-carcass roasts were reserved for the elite emperors, local street vendors mastered the art of using smaller, affordable cuts of mutton. By leveraging raw papaya as a powerful tenderizer, they transformed tough meat chunks into succulent, charcoal-smoked delicacies accessible to the common public during festive street markets.

## Ingredients
### Main Components
* **Mutton:** 500g boneless mutton cubes (cut from the shoulder, 1-inch pieces)
* **Tenderizer:** 2 tbsp Raw Papaya Paste (with skin)

### The First Marination
* **Nimbu Ras (Lemon Juice):** 1 tbsp
* **Namak (Salt):** 1 tsp
* **Adrak Paste (Ginger Paste):** 1 tbsp
* **Lahsun Paste (Garlic Paste):** 1 tbsp

### The Second Marination
* **Dahi (Thick Yogurt):** 1/2 cup, whisked
* **Kashmiri Lal Mirch Powder:** 1.5 tsp
* **Garam Masala Powder:** 1 tsp
* **Bhuna Jeera Powder (Roasted Cumin):** 1 tsp
* **Amchoor Powder (Dry Mango Powder):** 1/2 tsp
* **Kashmiri Kasuri Methi:** 1 tsp, crushed
* **Sarson Tel (Mustard Oil):** 2 tbsp

## Preparation
1. **Meat Preparation:** Wash the mutton boti thoroughly and pat completely dry with paper towels. 
2. **First Marination:** Rub the mutton pieces with raw papaya paste, lemon juice, salt, ginger, and garlic paste. Leave it in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours (overnight preferred) to allow the enzymes to break down the meat fibers.
3. **Second Marination:** In a large bowl, whisk the thick dahi with mustard oil, Kashmiri red chilli powder, garam masala, bhuna jeera, amchoor, and kasuri methi. Add the tenderized mutton to this mix and coat evenly. Let it rest for another 1 hour.

## Cooking Steps
1. **Skewering:** Remove the marinated mutton from the refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking to bring it to room temperature. Thread the boti tightly onto metal skewers, leaving a tiny gap between pieces for uniform heat distribution.
2. **Charcoal Grilling:** Prepare a traditional sigri or charcoal grill to medium-high heat. Place the skewers over the hot embers.
3. **Basting:** Grill the botis for about 12-15 minutes, rotating them frequently to ensure even cooking. Brush generous amounts of melted asli ghee or butter over the meat during the last 5 minutes of cooking.
4. **Resting:** Once the edges show a charred, dark brown crust and the meat feels soft to touch, remove from the grill. Let the kebabs rest on the skewer for 2 minutes before sliding them off onto a serving plate.

## When to Eat
* **Occasions:** Served as an excellent live-counter snack for winter barbecues, family celebrations, and outdoor evening gatherings.
* **Meal Timing:** Primarily enjoyed as a sunset or late-evening starter before a main course dinner.

## With What to Eat
* **Accompaniments:** Best eaten directly off the skewer with fluffy *Butter Naan* or *Mughlai Paratha*.
* **Condiments:** Serve hot alongside sliced red onions, fresh lemon wedges, and a sharp, spicy green chutney made of mint, coriander, and green chillies.

## Chef's Secrets
* **The Papaya Warning:** Never skip the raw papaya paste for Boti Kebab. Unlike minced meat, whole chunks of mutton will turn rubbery and tough over open charcoal heat without a strong fruit enzyme tenderizer.
* **The Mustard Oil Punch:** Heating the mustard oil slightly before adding it to the second marination unlocks a pungent aroma that binds perfectly with the charcoal smoke, mimicking a genuine restaurant-style clay tandoor flavor.
