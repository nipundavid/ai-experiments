# Shahi Mutton Stew
A aromatic, rustic, yet elegant Mughlai preparation, Shahi Mutton Stew (often called Ishtu) features tender mutton slow-cooked exclusively with a massive quantity of coarsely sliced onions, whole spices, and fresh aromatics, entirely excluding any fine ground spice powders.

## Place of Origin
Rampur and Lucknow, India.

## Backstory
Mutton Stew represents a unique confluence of historical British influence on Indian regional cooking during the colonial era, heavily adapted by the royal court kitchens of Rampur. While western stews relied on mild broth and boiled vegetables, Indian royal chefs transformed the concept by slow-braising the meat in its own juices along with high volumes of onions and whole spices (*Sabut Masala*). This allowed the dish to remain light and clean compared to dense kormas, yet packed with intense local flavor.

## Ingredients
### Main Components
* **Mutton:** 500g bone-in mutton pieces (preferably from the shoulder or neck)
* **Pyaz (Onions):** 4 large onions, coarsely sliced (stew requires a 1:1 or near 1:1 ratio of meat to onion)
* **Cooking Fat:** 4 tbsp Asli Ghee or refined oil

### Whole Spices (Sabut Masala)
* **Sabut Lal Mirch (Whole Dried Red Chillies):** 6 pieces, broken in half
* **Sabut Dhania (Whole Coriander Seeds):** 1.5 tsp, coarsely crushed
* **Kali Mirch (Black Peppercorns):** 1 tsp
* **Laung (Cloves):** 4 pieces
* **Choti Elaichi (Green Cardamom):** 4 pods
* **Dalchini (Cinnamon Stick):** 1-inch piece
* **Tejpatta (Bay Leaf):** 1 piece

### Fresh Aromatics & Dairy
* **Adrak (Ginger):** 2-inch piece, sliced into thin juliennes
* **Lahsun (Garlic):** 10-12 large cloves, coarsely chopped
* **Hari Mirch (Green Chillies):** 3-4, slit lengthwise
* **Dahi (Yogurt):** 1/2 cup, well-whisked
* **Tamatar (Tomato):** 1 small, chopped (optional, for acidity)

## Preparation
1. **Onion Slice:** Ensure the onions are cut into uniform, thick vertical slices rather than fine chops, as they need to melt down gradually during the slow-braising phase.
2. **Spice Prep:** Coarsely crush the whole coriander seeds using a mortar and pestle. Do not use fine pre-packaged powders.

## Cooking Steps
1. **Sautéing Aromatics:** Heat asli ghee in a heavy-bottomed handi or pressure cooker. Add all the whole spices (sabut lal mirch, crushed dhania, kali mirch, laung, elaichi, dalchini, tejpatta) and let them sizzle for 30 seconds.
2. **Adding Onions and Meat:** Add the chopped garlic, half of the ginger juliennes, and all the sliced onions. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until the onions become translucent but do not let them turn brown. Add the mutton pieces and sauté on high heat for 5 minutes until the meat sears.
3. **The Stewing Process:** Lower the heat. Add the whisked dahi, chopped tomato, slit green chillies, and salt. Mix thoroughly. Close the lid tightly. The onions and mutton will release an enormous amount of natural moisture—do not add extra water.
4. **Slow Cook / Pressure Cook:** Cook on low heat for 40 minutes (or 4-5 whistles in a pressure cooker) until the mutton meat separates easily from the bone.
5. **Bhunao (Reduction):** Open the lid. You will find a watery gravy with visible onion pieces. Turn the heat to medium-high and sauté continuously (*Bhunao*) for 10-12 minutes until the onions completely disintegrate into a thick, chunky, sweet-and-savory sauce and the ghee separates beautifully on top. Top with the remaining fresh ginger juliennes before serving.

## When to Eat
* **Occasions:** Perfect for casual family gatherings, rainy day lunches, or a lighter dinner choice when you want an authentic meat dish without the heaviness of ground spices.
* **Meal Timing:** Ideal for an early dinner or a relaxed weekend lunch.

## With What to Eat
* **Accompaniments:** Pairs spectacularly well with hot, crisp *Tandoori Roti*, layered *Lachha Paratha*, or plain hot steamed Basmati rice.
* **Condiments:** Serve with a simple green salad consisting of radishes, onions, and mint leaves with a dash of lime juice.

## Chef's Secrets
* **No Ground Spices Rule:** The visual character of a true Shahi Stew is white or pale yellow/translucent with flecks of red and green spices. Never add haldi (turmeric) or fine red chilli powder, as this converts the light stew profile into a standard commercial curry.
* **The Onion Sweetness Balance:** Because this dish utilizes a large amount of onions, it runs the risk of tasting overly sweet. To counter this, ensure you use high-quality whole dried red chillies and add a small splash of whisked yogurt or a tiny tomato near the end to bring the flavor profile back to a balanced savory note.
