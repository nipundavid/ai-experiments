# Shahi Mutton Galouti Kebab
The crown jewel of Awadhi gastronomy, Shahi Mutton Galouti Kebab is an ultra-fine, spice-imbued minced patty that is so soft and delicately structured that it literally melts away upon touching the tongue.

## Place of Origin
Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India.

## Backstory
Galouti translates directly to "melt-in-the-mouth." This extraordinary kebab was invented for the aging Nawab Asafo-ud-Daula of Lucknow. The Nawab was so deeply enamored with meat dishes that even when he completely lost his teeth, he refused to stop eating them. The royal khansamas engineered this hyper-soft kebab by mincing the meat up to 15 times, blending it with a secret mix of over 150 exotic spices, and utilizing high amounts of raw papaya to create an absolute paste-like texture that required no chewing whatsoever.

## Ingredients
### Main Components
* **Mutton:** 500g fine minced mutton (must contain 20% fat for proper lubrication)
* **Tenderizer:** 2.5 tbsp Raw Papaya Paste (ground smoothly with skin)

### The Galouti Secret Masala (Ground to a fine powder)
* **Shahi Jeera:** 1 tsp
* **Pathar ke Phool (Stone Flower):** 1 small piece
* **Khas Khas (Poppy Seeds):** 1 tsp
* **Choti Elaichi:** 4 pods
* **Javitri (Mace):** 1 blade
* **Jaiphal (Nutmeg):** 1/8th of a piece
* **Kali Mirch:** 8 pieces

### Binding & Aromatics
* **Pyaz Paste:** 2 tbsp (Fried onions ground into a smooth paste)
* **Adrak-Lahsun Paste:** 1.5 tbsp
* **Gram Flour (Besan):** 2 tbsp, lightly roasted
* **Asli Ghee:** For flat pan smoking and shallow frying
* **Kewra Water:** 1 tsp

## Preparation
1. **Hyper-Mincing:** Take your minced mutton and run it through a fine electric food processor or traditional sil-batta multiple times until the meat feels like a smooth, sticky dough with zero grains.
2. **Enzyme Breakdown:** Mix the fine meat paste with the raw papaya paste and salt. Let it sit inside the refrigerator for 4-5 hours. This intensive marination breaks down the meat structures completely.
3. **The Flavor Fold:** Add the freshly ground Galouti spice powder, fried onion paste, ginger-garlic paste, kewra water, and roasted roasted besan. Mix intensely with the heel of your hand for 10 minutes, almost like kneading a bread dough.

## Cooking Steps
1. **Dhungar (Charcoal Smoking):** Place the meat mixture in a wide bowl. Make a small well in the center and place a small steel cup or onion skin inside. Ignite a small piece of natural charcoal until red hot, place it in the cup, and pour 1 teaspoon of ghee over it. Immediately cover the large bowl with a lid, trapping the thick white smoke inside for 10 minutes to infuse the meat.
2. **Shaping:** The mixture will be incredibly sticky and loose. Wet your palms with a little water or ghee, take a small portion, and gently form a delicate, loose patty.
3. **Mahi Tawa Cooking:** Heat a generous amount of asli ghee on a wide, flat iron skillet (*Mahi Tawa*). Carefully slide the loose patties onto the skillet using a flat spatula.
4. **Frying:** Fry on medium-low heat for 3 minutes on each side. The exterior will develop a delicate, thin brown crust, while the interior remains cream-like. Flip with extreme care using a thin blade spatula, as the kebabs are prone to breaking.

## When to Eat
* **Occasions:** Served as an elite starter during high-end royal theme dinners, Eid banquets, and special milestone family celebrations.
* **Meal Timing:** Perfect as a late evening luxury appetizer.

## With What to Eat
* **Accompaniments:** Must be served exclusively with hot, saffron-flavored, layered saffron flatbread known as *Ulte Tawa ka Paratha* or traditional *Mughlai Paratha*.
* **Condiments:** Serve with fresh mint chutney and fine onion rings.

## Chef's Secrets
* **Fat Ratio Rule:** Never try to make Galouti Kebabs with completely lean, fat-free mutton. Without at least 20% natural fat integrated into the mince, the kebab will turn dry, chalky, and lose its iconic liquid-like melting texture on the pan.
* **The Smoking Secret:** The *Dhungar* (charcoal smoke) step is non-negotiable. It provides the deep, rustic flavor of an open wood fire while allowing you to cook the delicate, fragile patties safely on a flat iron pan.
