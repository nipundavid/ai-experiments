# Shahi Mutton Rezala
A stellar white-gravy masterpiece from the royal houses of Bengal, Shahi Mutton Rezala features tender meat braised in a highly aromatic, silky, and translucent sauce made from cashew paste, poppy seeds, and curd, accented with whole dried red chillies.

## Place of Origin
Kolkata, West Bengal, India.

## Backstory
Mutton Rezala marks a critical milestone in the evolution of late-stage Mughlai food. When the last Nawabs of Awadh (Lucknow) were exiled to Metiabruz in Kolkata by the British, their royal court chefs migrated with them. The chefs adapted their rich gravies to suit the local Bengali palate, dialing down heavy red spices and utilizing regional staples like poppy seeds (*Posto*). The result was a velvety white gravy that combined royal Awadhi complexity with Bengal's signature delicate sweetness.

## Ingredients
### Main Components
* **Mutton:** 750g bone-in mutton (preferably cleanly cut ribs and shoulder)
* **Fat Base:** 4 tbsp Asli Ghee

### The White Marination
* **Tok Doi (Sour Curd/Yogurt):** 1 cup, whisked completely smooth
* **Pyaz Paste (Onion Paste):** 1/2 cup (Boiled onions ground to a smooth paste)
* **Adrak-Lahsun Paste:** 1.5 tbsp
* **Namak (Salt):** 1 tsp

### The Shahi Paste (Nut Base)
* **Kaju (Cashew Nuts):** 12-15 pieces, soaked in warm milk
* **Posto (Poppy Seeds):** 1.5 tbsp, soaked in warm milk

### Whole Spices & Finishing Touch
* **Sabut Lal Mirch (Dry Red Chillies):** 5-6 pieces (kept whole for contrast)
* **Choti Elaichi (Green Cardamom):** 4 pods
* **Laung (Cloves):** 4 pieces
* **Dalchini (Cinnamon):** 1-inch stick
* **Kali Mirch Powder (White/Black Pepper):** 1 tsp
* **Makhana (Foxnuts):** 15 pieces (optional)
* **Meetha Attar:** 2 drops (Royal edible perfume)
* **Kewra Water:** 1 tsp

## Preparation
1. **Onion Paste Prep:** Always boil the onion slices in water for 5 minutes before grinding them into a paste. This eliminates the raw, pungent onion sulfur smell, ensuring a sweet, mellow base.
2. **Nut Blending:** Grind the soaked cashews and poppy seeds into an ultra-smooth, fine paste using a couple of spoons of milk.
3. **Marination:** Massage the mutton pieces with the smooth curd, boiled onion paste, ginger-garlic paste, and salt. Marinate in the refrigerator for a minimum of 3 hours.

## Cooking Steps
1. **Tempering Ghee:** Heat asli ghee in a heavy-bottomed wok or deep handi. Toss in the whole cinnamon, green cardamom, cloves, and whole dry red chillies. Sauté until the whole spices puff up and fill the kitchen with aroma.
2. **Braising:** Add the entire marinated mutton mixture along with the liquids directly into the pan. Cook on medium heat for 10-12 minutes, stirring constantly until the raw smell of the aromatics evaporates and the ghee separates onto the surface.
3. **Slow Simmer:** Add 1 cup of warm water, cover the vessel tightly with a heavy lid, and simmer on low heat for 45 minutes until the mutton pieces become fork-tender.
4. **Adding the White Paste:** Lower the heat to the absolute lowest setting. Pour in the blended cashew-poppy seed paste and white pepper powder. Mix gently. Cook uncovered for 8-10 minutes. Watch the gravy turn into a rich cream color. Drop in the crisp makhana balls during the last 5 minutes.
5. **Perfuming:** Finish by splashing the kewra water and exactly two drops of meetha attar. Shut the heat, trap the floral vapors for 5 minutes with a lid, and serve.

## When to Eat
* **Occasions:** A standout center-piece item for elite dinner parties, special family celebrations, and formal celebrations where guests appreciate subtle, non-spicy aromatic gravies.
* **Meal Timing:** Pairs excellently as a luxurious dinner dish.

## With What to Eat
* **Accompaniments:** Best enjoyed with sweet, saffron-tinged *Sheermal*, soft *Rumali Roti*, or pristine white *Basmati Pulao*.
* **Condiments:** Serve alongside mild onion salads. Avoid spicy green chutneys, as they overpower the delicate, perfumed profile of the rezala.

## Chef's Secrets
* **Keep Chillies Whole:** The dried red chillies in a Rezala are intended for a visual splash of crimson contrast against a white canvas and to provide a mild smoky flavor. Never break or tear them open during cooking; doing so releases seeds that will discolor your pristine white gravy and make it overly spicy.
* **The Attar Warning:** Meetha Attar is an incredibly potent, concentrated oil-based extract. Do not mistake it for standard rose water. A single drop too many can completely ruin the entire handi of Rezala by creating a soapy, synthetic perfume taste. Stick strictly to 1-2 drops.
